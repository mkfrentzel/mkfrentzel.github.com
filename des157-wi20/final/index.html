News Exposure on Social Media

According to data by the PEW Research Center, news content on social media pages tend to lean on the more liberal side. Does this make it fake? Definitely not! But the more content available, the more opportunities for false information to be present. Be aware and be cautious.

Age Matters

It's well known that the younger generation tends to use social media platforms more frequently and with more ease compared to those who came before them. But with more exposure it only becomes more important to stay alert when sifting through news social media sites - targeted ads and propoganda seek out the vulnerable and it's far easier to be swayed into believing something when you've seen it repeatedly or frequently.

Digital News vs IRL

As digitalization has taken over, many print industries have been hit hard and the print publication system as a whole is rapidly declining. While the internet is overflowing with resources, information, and different types of content, it can be a dangerous place. The more information available, the harder it becomes to differentiate what's fact and what's false, especially in computer mediated formats.

Questions
To Ask

Who created it?

Check your sources - .com or .net sources aren't always the most reliable. Dig around the site and try and find out more about the author and organization. Are there any obvious biases? Are their sources credible?

What is the message?

Are you shocked by the headline? Can the same information be found from multiple sources? Is it based in fact or opinion? Does it take a neutral perspective?

Why does it exist?

Is it a sponsored or paid advertisement? Does it intend to put down another group or viewpoint? What is the purpose of the text - to help inform or to harm?
