THE HISTORY OF

FAKE NEWS

1755

It played a role in catalyzing the Enlightenment, when the Catholic Church’s false explanation of the 1755 Lisbon Earthquake prompted Voltaire to speak out against religious dominance.

1898

In the 1890s, rival newspaper publishers Joseph Pulitzer and William Hearst competed over the audience through sensationalism and reporting rumors as though they were facts, a practice that became known at the time as “yellow journalism.” Their incredulous news played a role in leading the US into the Spanish-American War of 1898.

News Exposure on Social Media

According to data by the PEW Research Center, news content on social media pages tend to lean on the more liberal side. Does this make it fake? Definitely not! But the more content available, the more opportunities for false information to be present. Be aware and be cautious.

Questions
To Ask

Who created it?

Check your sources - .com or .net sources aren't always the most reliable. Dig around the site and try and find out more about the author and organization. Are there any obvious biases? Are their sources credible?

What is the message?

Are you shocked by the headline? Can the same information be found from multiple sources? Is it based in fact or opinion? Does it take a neutral perspective?

Why does it exist?

Is it a sponsored or paid advertisement? Does it intend to put down another group or viewpoint? What is the purpose of the text - to help inform or to harm?
